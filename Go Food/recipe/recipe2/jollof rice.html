Jollof Rice

How To Make?

Ingredents Needed

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended (or 1 cup tomato puree)
  • 3 red bell peppers, blended
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to spice preference), blended
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon butter (optional, for extra flavor)
First Step!
Prepare the base:
In a blender, combine tomatoes, red bell peppers, and scotch bonnet peppers. Blend until smooth.
Second Step!
Fry the sauce:
Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until soft. Add minced garlic and ginger; sauté for 1 minute. Pour in the blended tomato mixture and cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens and the oil floats to the top.
Third Step!
Season the sauce:
Add thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir and let it simmer for another 5 minutes.
Fourth Step!
Add the rice:
Rinse the rice thoroughly and add it to the pot. Stir well to coat the rice in the sauce.
Fifth Step!
Cook the rice:
Add the chicken or vegetable stock (it should cover the rice). Stir once, cover the pot with a lid, and reduce the heat to low. Let it cook for 20-30 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy. Check occasionally and add a bit more stock if needed.
Sixth Step!
Finishing touches:
When the rice is done, stir in the butter (optional) for extra richness. Remove bay leaves and fluff the rice with a fork.
Seventh Step!
Serve:
Serve hot with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or fish, and enjoy!
Enjoy!!
